Using the powers granted under the Freedom of Information Act, please may I request the following Data to broken down annually since 2010 and up to the latest available quarter.

(1) How many offenders, released from custody on licence and supervised by probation providers in the UK, were convicted of a Serious Further Offence during the period that they were on licence of within 28 days of completing a period under licence of:

(A) Murder
(B) Manslaughter

(2) How many SFO convictions resulting from SFO notifications received between 1 January 2015 and up to the latest available point in time, where an offender released from custody on licence and supervised by probation providers in England and Wales was convicted of a SFO of murder or manslaughter during the period that they were under licence or within 28 days of completing a period under licence, and was either classified as a Prolific and Priority Offender (PPO) or managed under IOM at the time of the SFO.

(3) How many SFO convictions resulting from SFO notifications received between 1 January 2015 and up to the latest available point in time, where an offender released from custody on licence and supervised (or within 28 days of completing a period under licence supervision) by probation providers in England and Wales was convicted of a SFO of murder or manslaughter, and the index offence(s) was one of violence against a person.

(4) How many SFO convictions resulting from SFO notifications received between 1 January 2015 and up to the latest available point in time, where an offender released from custody on licence and supervised (or within 28 days of completing a period under licensed supervision) by probation providers in England and Wales was convicted of a SFO of murder or manslaughter, and was identified as having a high or very high risk of reoffending.

(5) How many SFO allegations of murder or manslaughter were received between 1 January 2015 and up to the latest available point in time, where an offender released from custody on licence and supervised (or within 28 days of completing a period under licensed supervision) by probation providers in England and Wales.
